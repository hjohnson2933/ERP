## The estimation model (as built)

- **Part** = a material in `erp.materials` (the simplest unit).
- **Assembly** = a named build defined by a **Bill of Materials** (BOM) — a list of parts
  and/or **child assemblies** (sub-assemblies), each with a quantity. Cost **rolls up**
  recursively from material costs. Circular BOMs are rejected by a DB trigger.
- **Fixture** = a finished assembly (`is_fixture`) assigned to exactly **one program**.
- **Program** = a brand's set of active fixtures.
- **Estimate** = a collection of **fixtures** (priced at rolled-up cost × markup) plus
  **custom lines**. One estimate-wide **markup %** with **per-line overrides**.
- **Price lock-in:** submitting an estimate freezes its pricing into a **snapshot**;
  re-pricing takes a new snapshot and compares to the previous one.

### Design decisions made this session
- **1B** — programs/assemblies are **new tables**, kept separate from the existing
  `order_forms`/`order_form_items` (which still back the Orders module).
- **2C** — estimate-wide markup **with per-line override**.
- **3A** — estimate lines are **fixtures + custom** (materials live inside fixtures now).
- **4A** — **one program per fixture**.
- **Price lock** on submit; re-price re-snapshots so old vs new can be compared.
- The earlier "materials only store cost, not sell price" concern is **resolved** by the
  markup model (cost rolls up; markup produces sell price).

## Open items / next steps

1. **Estimate → Order conversion** (deliberately not built). Orders still use the old
   `order_forms`/`order_form_items` model while estimates now use fixtures/programs.
   Wiring "accept → create order" needs a design decision on how orders look under the new
   model. This is the natural next project.
2. **Legacy estimate lines:** any *material* lines created before the Stage 4 rework will
   convert to *custom* lines (keeping their then-current price) the next time such an
   estimate is edited and saved.
3. **BOM override scope:** a cost override lives on the BOM line (per-assembly), not on the
   material — it does not change the material's catalog cost. A "material-wide override"
   would be a separate feature.
4. From the 2026-07-10 handoff, still relevant: `customers`/`estimates`/`materials` domain
   schemas were originally deferred pending iPOL/Sage/ProjectPAK exports; the estimation
   side is now built out from the ERP's own model rather than those exports.
